Author Events

Engaging book discussions, readings, and writing workshops for readers and aspiring authors.

Bring an Author to Your Community

Whether you're hosting a book club, library event, school visit, or community reading, Dr. Danny Gentry creates engaging experiences that go beyond the typical author appearance. With three published books spanning fiction, education, and children's literature, he offers rich material for discussion and brings a storyteller's passion to every event.

Event Types

Book Club Discussions

Author Readings & Signings

Writing Workshops

Interactive sessions on craft, storytelling, and the writing life. Suitable for aspiring authors and creative writing students.

School & Library Visits

Age-appropriate presentations about storytelling, the writing process, and the journey from idea to published book.

Panel Discussions

Join Dr. Gentry alongside other authors for discussions on education, publishing, or the craft of writing.

Virtual Events

Featured Books for Discussion

The Serpent of Houston Creek

Fiction / Mystery
Perfect for book clubs who enjoy atmospheric mysteries with strong Texas settings. Discussions often center on community dynamics, the nature of secrets, and how place shapes character. Ideal for groups of 8-30 participants.

The Collapse: How America Abandoned Its Teachers—and What Comes Next

Non-Fiction / Education
Powerful material for educator groups, parent organizations, and community conversations about public education. Generates deep discussion about policy, profession, and the future of schools. Best for audiences invested in educational issues.

The Kindness Tree

Children's Literature
Beautiful for elementary school visits, family literacy events, and children's book clubs. Includes activities and discussion questions about kindness, character, and making positive choices. Ages 6-10 ideal, but enjoyed by all ages.

Event Formats

Intimate Book Club (45-60 minutes)

Perfect for groups of 8-20. Deep-dive discussion with plenty of time for questions and personal connection.

Community Event (60-90 minutes)

Larger gatherings with reading, presentation, Q&A, and signing. Ideal for libraries, bookstores, and community centers.

Writing Workshop (2-3 hours)

Interactive sessions on craft, with exercises and feedback. For aspiring writers and creative writing students.

School Visit (Full or Half Day)

Multiple presentations for different age groups, classroom visits, and time with student writers. Customized to your school's needs.
